Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions regarding cybersecurity and safety of your data online:

1. What does protecting my personal data mean and why does it matter?

Safeguarding your private digital information, accounts, and devices from unauthorized access. As more of life moves online, data security is essential to avoid fraud, theft, stalking and other issues.

2. What are the main threats to my personal data security right now?

Phishing scams, computer infections, corporations with data breaches, hackers guessing passwords, public WiFi snooping, and shady apps capturing user data are major threats that can access private files and accounts.

3. What are quick steps I can take to better protect my data?

  • Use a password manager
  • Turn on two-factor authentication
  • Be wary of phishing attempts
  • Only download software from official sources and keep everything updated
  • Use a VPN when connected to public WiFi

4. How do smart home devices like virtual assistants impact my personal data protections?

Smart home devices collect incredible private data and get frequently targeted by hackers. Tweaking privacy settings, using complex passphrases, updating firmware, and limiting financial info access can help reduce, but not eliminate risks which require ongoing mindfulness.

5. How do social media and mobile apps access and utilize my private data?

Social media platforms and smartphone apps, even basic games and utilities, often collect vast amounts of personal data from users, ranging from location traces, contacts, messages, photos, search histories, biometric data, behavioral usage patterns and more. This data allows companies to train algorithms and target advertising, but it can also expose sensitive information if improperly secured.
